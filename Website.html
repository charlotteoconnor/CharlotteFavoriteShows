Charlotte's Favorite Shows

Grey's Anatomy

Where to watch: Netflix

Grey's Anatomy is a long-running American medical drama television series that premiered on March 27, 2005, on ABC, created by Shonda Rhimes. The show revolves around the personal and professional life of Meredith Grey and her colleagues at the fictional Grey Sloan Memorial Hospital in Seattle, exploring complex themes like relationships, ethics, and mortality, often incorporating real-world medical cases and issues. With a diverse ensemble cast, including Ellen Pompeo, Sandra Oh, and Chandra Wilson, among others, Grey's Anatomy has received widespread critical acclaim and won numerous awards, including Primetime Emmy Awards, Golden Globe Awards, and People's Choice Awards.

Never Have I Ever

Where to watch: Netflix

Never Have I Ever is a Netflix original series that follows the life of Devi Vishwakumar, an Indian-American high school student, as she navigates her way through the challenges of adolescence, family, and friendships after the sudden death of her father. The show is a comedy-drama that explores themes of identity, grief, and coming-of-age, featuring a talented ensemble cast, including Maitreyi Ramakrishnan, Poorna Jagannathan, and Richa Moorjani. It has received critical acclaim for its portrayal of the South Asian experience and its exploration of complex themes, and has been praised for its representation of diverse characters and nuanced exploration of the high school experience, with four seasons available, including the final season released on June 8, 2023.

The Legend of Korra

Where to watch: Netflix

The Legend of Korra is a Nickelodeon animated series that takes place about 70 years after the events of Avatar: The Last Airbender. The show follows the journey of Korra, the new Avatar, a young and feisty Water Tribe warrior who must master the four elements - air, water, earth, and fire - to maintain balance and harmony in a rapidly changing world. As Korra navigates the challenges of being the Avatar, she faces various enemies, including the anti-bender movement led by Amon, and later, the Red Lotus, all while exploring themes of identity, power, and the consequences of one's actions, set against the backdrop of a steampunk-inspired, industrialized world.

Ginny & Georgia

Where to watch: Netflix

Ginny & Georgia is a Netflix original series that follows the complex and often tumultuous relationship between Ginny Miller, a teenager navigating high school and adolescence, and her mother Georgia, a single parent with a mysterious and troubled past. As they settle into a new life in the small town of Wellsbury, Massachusetts, Ginny struggles to balance her own identity and relationships with her mother's secrets and lies, while Georgia's past begins to unravel, threatening to upend their lives and relationships with those around them, exploring themes of family, trauma, and the complexities of mother-daughter relationships.

Heartstopper

Where to watch: Netflix

Heartstopper is a Netflix original series that follows the story of Charlie Spring, a gay schoolboy who falls in love with his classmate Nick Nelson, and explores their journey of self-discovery, friendship, and romance. The show is a heart warming and authentic portrayal of teenage life, tackling themes of identity, sexuality, and mental health, with a strong focus on the importance of empathy, kindness, and acceptance, featuring a talented young cast, including Kit Connor and Joe Locke.

Abbott Elemtary

Where to watch: MAX

Abbott Elementary is a mockumentary-style sitcom that follows the lives of a dedicated and passionate group of teachers at Abbott Elementary, a underfunded and understaffed public school in Philadelphia. The show, created by Quinta Brunson, stars Brunson as Janine Teagues, a second-grade teacher, alongside a talented ensemble cast, including Tyler James Williams, Janelle James, and Lisa Ann Walter, as they navigate the challenges of educating their students and dealing with the bureaucratic red tape, all while maintaining their sense of humor and commitment to their students, offering a heartwarming and hilarious portrayal of the unsung heroes of the education system.

BuzzFeed Unsolved

Where to watch: YouTube

BuzzFeed Unsolved is a popular true crime and paranormal investigation series that delves into a wide range of mysterious and often eerie cases, from famous murders and unexplained events to ghostly encounters and supernatural legends. Hosted by Ryan Bergara and Shane Madej, the show combines meticulous research, witty banter, and a healthy dose of skepticism as they explore the darker side of human nature and the unknown, often incorporating comedic elements and pop culture references, making the show both entertaining and thought-provoking.

Young Sheldon

Where to watch: Netflix

Young Sheldon is a spin-off of the popular sitcom The Big Bang Theory, following the childhood of Sheldon Cooper, a brilliant and eccentric nine-year-old boy growing up in a small town in Texas in the late 1980s. The show, created by Chuck Lorre and Steven Molaro, explores Sheldon's struggles to navigate high school, his family dynamics, and his own unique perspective on the world, all while showcasing his exceptional intelligence and wit, as he navigates adolescence and learns to cope with the challenges of being a gifted but socially awkward child.
